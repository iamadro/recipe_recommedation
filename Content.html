Item

  • Laddu
  • Ingredients
    • 2 cups besan (chickpea flour)
    • 1 cup powdered sugar
    • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
    • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
    • A handful of chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
    • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
    • 1-2 tablespoons milk (if using saffron)
  • Recipe
    1. Heat a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai on low to medium heat and add the ghee. Let the ghee melt completely.
    2. Add the besan (chickpea flour) to the melted ghee and roast it on low heat. Stir continuously to avoid lumps and to ensure even roasting. The besan will change color and emit a nutty aroma. This process takes about 15-20 minutes. Be patient, and don't rush the roasting process. You'll know it's ready when the besan turns a golden brown color.
    3. Turn off the heat and let the roasted besan cool down to room temperature. During this time, you can also warm the saffron strands in a couple of tablespoons of milk, which will infuse the saffron color and flavor into the laddu.
    4. Once the roasted besan has cooled down, add the powdered sugar and cardamom powder. You can also add the saffron-infused milk at this stage.
    5. Mix everything together until it forms a crumbly mixture. If the mixture is too dry, you can add a little more ghee to bind it.
    6. Now, add the chopped nuts and mix them into the mixture.
    7. Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into round laddus with your hands. The laddus should hold their shape.
    8. Repeat this process until all the mixture is used up.
    9. Let the laddus cool completely before storing them in an airtight container. They will firm up as they cool down.
